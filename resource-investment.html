Our Approach

Evidence
over
noise.

We don't chase performance. We build portfolios that capture what markets reliably offer — diversified, cost-efficient, behaviorally sound.

01

Markets are efficient

Most active managers underperform their benchmark after fees. We accept this reality rather than paying for underperformance. Passive and factor-based strategies form the bedrock of every portfolio we build.

02

Diversification is the only free lunch

Spreading risk across thousands of securities, sectors, and geographies reduces volatility without sacrificing return. No single company, sector, or country bet is worth the concentration risk it introduces.

03

Behavior is the x-factor

The gap between investment returns and investor returns exists because of behavior — panic selling, performance chasing, overconfidence. Our job is to keep you invested through volatility with a plan built for exactly that purpose.

04

Tax efficiency compounds

Where you hold assets matters as much as what you hold. Asset location, tax-loss harvesting, and Roth conversion strategy can add meaningful after-tax return with no additional market risk.

Portfolio Building Blocks

What we
invest in.

Each asset class serves a specific role. Together, they form a portfolio designed to capture return where it exists and buffer against volatility where it matters.

01
US Equities
Growth engine

Broad US market exposure through low-cost index funds and factor-tilted strategies. The primary growth engine of most long-horizon portfolios, diversified across thousands of companies.

02
International Equities
Geographic diversification

Developed and emerging market exposure provides geographic diversification and access to growth cycles that don't always correlate with the US market.

03
Fixed Income
Stability & ballast

High-quality bonds reduce portfolio volatility and provide a buffer in equity drawdowns. Duration and credit quality are calibrated to your time horizon and income needs.

04
Real Assets
Inflation hedge

REITs and commodity exposure provide inflation protection and low correlation to stocks and bonds. A modest allocation improves portfolio resilience across market regimes.

05
Factor Strategies
Evidence-based tilts

Value, profitability, and small-cap factor tilts have demonstrated persistent, long-horizon return premiums backed by decades of academic research across global markets.

06
Cash & Equivalents
Liquidity & optionality

Strategic cash reserves serve short-term needs and opportunity deployment. Money markets and short-term treasuries now offer meaningful yields, making cash a productive asset class in its own right.

Discuss Our Approach
Questions

Investing,
answered.

These are the questions clients ask most often — before they invest, during volatility, and as their wealth grows. Answered plainly, without jargon.

Your situation always calls for a personal conversation.

The question is almost always the wrong one. Research consistently shows that time in the market outperforms timing the market. An investor who waited for "the right moment" across the past century would have been sitting in cash through most of the best decades. The right time to invest is when you have capital aligned with a long-horizon plan — which is now, for most clients.

Volatility is the price of admission for long-term returns. The S&P 500 has experienced a 10%+ drawdown in roughly 1 out of every 2 years — yet has delivered positive returns in roughly 3 out of every 4 calendar years. Our job is to build a portfolio you can stay invested in, even when it's uncomfortable — and to give you a behavioral plan before the discomfort arrives.

Active managers attempt to select securities that will outperform the market. Studies show that over 80–90% of active large-cap managers underperform their benchmark net of fees over 15-year periods. Passive strategies instead capture the market's return at minimal cost. Factor-based ("smart beta") strategies occupy a middle ground — passive in execution but tilted toward return premiums supported by evidence.

There's no universal answer — but the key variables are time horizon and behavioral risk tolerance. A 35-year-old with a 30-year runway can absorb more equity volatility than a 60-year-old entering retirement. Rule-of-thumb formulas like "100 minus your age" in bonds are outdated given longer life expectancies and lower bond yields. Your allocation should be stress-tested against real worst-case scenarios, not just averages.

A 1% annual fee difference may seem small — but over 30 years on a $500,000 portfolio growing at 7%, that gap represents over $500,000 in lost wealth. We use institutional-class and index fund vehicles wherever possible, targeting blended expense ratios well below 0.20%. Advisory fees are transparent and disclosed — never embedded in products or commissions.
