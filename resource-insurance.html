Life Insurance
Coverage amount reviewed — face value aligned to income replacement needs (typically 10–15× income)
Policy type confirmed — term vs. permanent evaluated based on need and budget
Beneficiary designations current — primary and contingent named
Ownership reviewed — consider ILIT if estate tax is a concern
Disability & Income Protection
Long-term disability coverage — own-occupation definition; covers 60–70% of income
Benefit period sufficient — to age 65 minimum for high-income earners
Short-term disability or emergency fund covers the elimination period
Property & Liability
Homeowners/renters policy reviewed — replacement cost, not actual cash value
Auto liability limits reviewed — minimum $100K/$300K recommended
Personal umbrella policy — $1M+ coverage for high-net-worth households
Long-Term Care & Health
Long-term care strategy in place — insurance, self-insure plan, or hybrid policy
Health insurance adequate — review deductibles, out-of-pocket max, network
HSA maximized if enrolled in HDHP ($4,400 individual / $8,750 family in 2026)
Key-person insurance reviewed if business owner
Annual insurance review scheduled — coverages grow stale as life changes
